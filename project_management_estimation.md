# 📘 Estimating Time and Cost – Study Notes

## ⏱️💰 Why Estimation Matters

Two of the most common project questions:
- "How long will it take?"
- "How much will it cost?"

✅ Accurate estimates are critical because they influence:
- Project approval decisions
- Scheduling
- Budget planning

---

## 🔄 Where Estimation Happens

### 📌 During Initiating & Planning
- Initial estimates created with a **core planning team**
- Estimates are rough and less accurate

### 📌 During Execution
- More accurate estimates from:
  - Team members doing the work
- Based on real experience and task understanding

---

## 📊 Improving Estimate Accuracy

- Early stage → ±75% accuracy is acceptable  
- Later stages → Aim for ±10% accuracy  

📌 Accuracy improves as:
- More information becomes available
- Project scope becomes clearer

---

## 🔢 What to Estimate

### 🔹 1. Time
- Determines the project schedule
- Impacts overall cost

### 🔹 2. Cost
- Labor (time-based)
- Materials
- Additional costs (e.g., travel, tools)

---

## 🛠️ Estimation Techniques

### 📌 1. Analogous Estimating
- Use data from **similar past projects**
- Quick and simple

✅ Best for:
- Early-stage estimates

---

### 📌 2. Parametric Estimating
- Use measurable units (e.g., cost per square foot)
- Based on historical data and formulas

✅ Best for:
- Repetitive, data-driven projects

---

### 📌 3. Expert Judgment
- Consult:
  - Experienced professionals
  - Vendors or consultants

✅ Best for:
- New or unfamiliar projects

---

### 📌 4. Delphi Technique
- Multiple experts estimate **independently**
- Estimates are:
  - Shared anonymously
  - Revised over multiple rounds
- Final estimate = average of last round

✅ Benefit:
- Reduces bias from authority or group influence

---

### 📌 5. Top-Down Estimating
- Start with:
  - Overall project or major phases
- Break down into smaller parts

✅ Best for:
- Large projects
- Early rough estimates

---

### 📌 6. Bottom-Up Estimating
- Estimate **individual tasks**
- Add them up for total project estimate

✅ Best for:
- Detailed and accurate planning

---

### 🔄 Combined Approach
- Start with **top-down**
- Refine using **bottom-up**

✅ Provides balance of speed and accuracy

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> Estimating starts rough and becomes more precise over time—accuracy improves as the project becomes better understood.

---

## 🚀 Key Takeaways

- Start with **time estimation**, then derive cost
- Use different techniques depending on:
  - Project phase
  - Available data
- Combine methods for better accuracy
- Involve team members for realistic estimates
- Continuously refine estimates throughout the project

---
# 📘 Choosing the Best Estimate – Study Notes

## 🎯 The Goal of Selecting an Estimate

Choosing the right estimate is about:
- ✅ Maximizing the probability of success
- ✅ Providing realistic and achievable timelines
- ✅ Balancing risk and customer expectations

---

## 🚗 Real-World Analogy (Commute Time)

- Best case → 30 minutes (rare)
- Typical (average) → 45 minutes
- Worst case → 60 minutes (traffic, delays)

✅ You don’t choose the average or best case —  
➡️ You choose a **safe estimate with buffer**

---

## 📊 Understanding the Bell Curve

Project estimates often follow a **normal distribution (bell curve)**:

- 🔽 50% of values are **below the average**
- 🔼 50% are **above the average**

### ⚠️ Problem with Average Estimate:
- Only **50% chance of success**
- Too risky for projects

---

## ❌ Estimates to Avoid

### 🚫 Best Case Estimate
- Assumes everything goes perfectly
- Sets unrealistic expectations
- High probability of failure

---

### 🚫 Worst Case Estimate
- Very high probability of success
- But:
  - Too pessimistic
  - May cause project rejection

---

### 🚫 Most Likely (Average) Estimate
- 50/50 chance of success
- Too risky for professional commitments

---

## ✅ Recommended Approach

### 📌 Use a "Safe Estimate"

➡️ Choose a value:
> **Halfway between the average and the worst-case estimate**

---

## 📈 Why This Works

- Provides approximately **86% probability of success**
- Balances:
  - ✅ Realism
  - ✅ Risk management
  - ✅ Customer acceptance

---

## ⚙️ Adjusting Your Estimate

You can tweak your estimate based on context:

### 🔹 Increase Estimate (More Buffer)
- High-risk projects
- Critical deadlines
- Important stakeholders

✅ Reduces risk of missing deadlines

---

### 🔹 Decrease Estimate (More Competitive)
- Trying to win a project
- Accepting higher risk

⚠️ Increases chance of failure

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> The best estimate is not the fastest or the safest—it’s the one with an acceptable probability of success.

---

## 🚀 Key Takeaways

- Avoid **best case, worst case, and average-only estimates**
- Use a **risk-balanced estimate (~86% success probability)**
- Base decisions on **probability, not optimism**
- Adjust estimates depending on **risk tolerance and context**
