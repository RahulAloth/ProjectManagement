# 📘 Study Notes: Planning an Iteration (Agile – Speculate Stage)

## 🔹 1. Purpose of Iteration Planning
- In Agile, planning happens at the **start of each iteration**
- This occurs during the **Speculate stage**

✅ Goal:
- Decide **what work (features)** will be completed in the upcoming iteration

---

## 🔹 2. What Is a Feature?
- A **feature** is:
  - A small, usable function or deliverable
  - That satisfies a **business need**

### Naming Convention:
- **Action + Result**

### Examples:
- Find next available appointment time  
- Change procedure room  
- Generate billing report  

---

## 🔹 3. Building the Backlog

### ✅ Backlog:
- A **complete list of all project features**

### How to create:
- Write features on:
  - Sticky notes / index cards  
  - Agile tools (e.g., Jira, Azure DevOps)

---

### ⚠️ First Iteration Note:
- Takes **more time** because:
  - Entire backlog must be:
    - Identified
    - Estimated

---

## 🔹 4. Prioritizing Features

### 👥 Business Team:
- Prioritizes based on:
  - Business value
  - Functionality importance

### 👨‍💻 Technical Team:
- Considers:
  - Technical dependencies
  - Logical build sequence

✅ Result:
- A **prioritized backlog**

---

## 🔹 5. Estimation of Features
- Business and technical teams:
  - Collaborate to estimate:
    - Effort required
    - Complexity

✅ Output:
- Effort estimates for all features

---

## 🔹 6. Handling Changes
- Agile welcomes:
  - New features
  - Modifications

### If backlog becomes too large:
- Move less important features to:
  - Future iterations or projects

---

## 🔹 7. Creating the Iteration Plan

Once backlog is prioritized and approved:

### Build:
- **Iteration Plan**
- **Milestone Plan**
- **Release Plan**

### Includes:
- Which features:
  - Go into each iteration
  - Will be delivered and when

---

## 🔹 8. Starting the Iteration
- After planning:
  - Move to **Explore stage**
  - Begin development and testing

---

## 🔹 9. Iteration-to-Iteration Planning

Before each new iteration:

✅ Re-evaluate:
- Feature priorities
- Effort estimates

✅ Include:
- Incomplete features (returned to backlog)
- Newly identified features
- Next highest priority items

---

## 🔹 10. Timebox for Speculate Stage
- Short planning duration

### Example:
- 3-week iteration → ~2 days planning

✅ Keeps Agile:
- Fast
- Flexible
- Efficient

---

## 🔹 ✅ Key Takeaways
- Iteration planning = **Speculate stage**
- Focuses on selecting and planning **features**
- Backlog is:
  - Created, prioritized, and continuously updated
- Planning is:
  - Short and repeated every iteration
- Agile adapts by:
  - Re-prioritizing and re-estimating frequently

---
# 📘 Study Notes: Handling the Adapt and Close Stages (Agile)

## 🔹 1. Adapt Stage (End of Each Iteration)

### 🎯 Purpose:
- Review results, reflect on performance, and improve future iterations

---

## 🔹 2. Key Activities in the Adapt Stage

### ✅ 2.1 Compare Planned vs Delivered
- Evaluate:
  - What was planned
  - What was actually delivered

---

### ✅ 2.2 Team Reflection
- Discuss:
  - ✅ What worked well
  - ❌ What didn’t work
- Identify improvements for future iterations

---

### ✅ 2.3 Customer Review
- Validate completed features:
  - Are they working correctly?
  - Do they deliver expected business value?

---

### ✅ 2.4 Lessons Learned Session
- Conduct team discussion to:
  - Identify problems
  - Remove obstacles
  - Improve processes
- Capture:
  - Successes to repeat
  - Issues to fix

---

## 🔹 3. Outcomes of the Adapt Stage

### 🔁 Feed into Next Iteration (Speculate Stage)

Possible updates include:
- Add/remove features in backlog
- Reprioritize features
- Adjust effort estimates
- Update risk register
- Modify team composition
- Improve processes

---

## 🔹 4. Additional Benefits
- ✅ Continuous improvement
- ✅ Better alignment with customer needs
- ✅ Stronger team collaboration

---

## 🔹 5. Team Well-being
- Adapt stage is also a time to:
  - Celebrate achievements 🎉
  - Take short breaks
  - Recharge before next iteration

---

## 🔹 6. Transition After Adapt Stage

### If work remains:
    Adapt → Next Iteration (Speculate)

### If project is complete:
    Adapt → Close Phase

---

## 🔹 7. Close Stage (End of Project)

### 🎯 Purpose:
- Finalize and formally complete the project

---

## 🔹 8. Key Activities in the Close Stage

### ✅ 8.1 Financial Closure
- Ensure:
  - All invoices are paid
  - Project accounts are closed

---

### ✅ 8.2 Resource Reassignment
- Work with managers to:
  - Reassign team members to new tasks/projects

---

### ✅ 8.3 Final Communication
- Share:
  - Project results
  - Outcomes with stakeholders and customers

---

### ✅ 8.4 Final Lessons Learned
- Document:
  - Key insights
  - Best practices for future projects

---

## 🔹 9. Importance of Closure
- Confirms:
  - Project success
- Prevents:
  - Loose ends
  - Resource misallocation
- Improves:
  - Future project execution

---

## 🔹 ✅ Key Takeaways
- **Adapt stage** focuses on:
  - Review, feedback, and continuous improvement
- Drives updates for future iterations
- Encourages:
  - Learning
  - Team collaboration
- **Close stage** ensures:
  - Proper project completion
  - Financial and operational closure
- Both stages are critical for:
  - Sustained project success
  - Organizational learning

# 📘 Study Notes: Integrating Agile and Waterfall in Hybrid Projects

## 🔹 1. What Is a Hybrid Project?
- A **hybrid project** combines:
  - ✅ Waterfall approach (structured, sequential)
  - ✅ Agile approach (iterative, flexible)

✅ Challenge:
- Ensuring both approaches **work together smoothly**

---

## 🔹 2. Core Integration Principle
- Use **Waterfall as the main framework**
- Treat Agile work as **components within the overall plan**

---

## 🔹 3. Key Integration Strategies

### ✅ 3.1 Use Waterfall as the Primary Path
- Build the main schedule using:
  - Waterfall structure
- Agile efforts fit **within this framework**

---

### ✅ 3.2 Treat Agile Work as Tasks
- Represent Agile sub-projects as:
  - **Longer-duration tasks** in the Waterfall schedule

✅ Benefit:
- Keeps overall schedule **organized and visible**

---

### ✅ 3.3 Track Agile Progress in Waterfall Schedule
- Define a process to:
  - Capture Agile progress
  - Report status within the Waterfall plan

✅ Ensures:
- Stakeholder visibility
- Alignment across teams

---

### ✅ 3.4 Integrate Features Incrementally
- Do NOT deliver all Agile features at once

👉 Instead:
- Integrate **features one at a time**

✅ Benefits:
- Easier:
  - Testing
  - Debugging
  - Issue resolution

---

### ✅ 3.5 Allocate Time for Integration Work
- Ensure Agile team members:
  - Participate in:
    - Integration tasks
    - Testing activities

✅ Prevents:
- Bottlenecks
- Misalignment between teams

---

### ✅ 3.6 Allow Time Between Integrations
- Schedule gaps between feature integrations

✅ Why?
- Avoid:
  - Overlapping testing
  - Increased complexity
- Enable:
  - Proper validation and correction

---

### ✅ 3.7 Review Impact Before Reprioritizing Agile Work
- Before changing Agile backlog priorities:
  - Check impact on **Waterfall schedule**

✅ Ensures:
- No disruption to overall project timeline

---

### ✅ 3.8 Define Roles and Responsibilities Clearly
- Senior roles (e.g., Product Owner) must:
  - Understand integration approach
  - Participate in both:
    - Agile sub-projects
    - Waterfall coordination

✅ Importance:
- Aligns decision-making across methodologies

---

## 🔹 4. Key Risks to Manage
- Misalignment between Agile and Waterfall schedules
- Overlapping integrations causing confusion
- Lack of coordination between teams
- Uncontrolled backlog changes affecting timeline

---

## 🔹 5. Benefits of Proper Integration
- ✅ Smooth coordination between teams
- ✅ Better schedule control
- ✅ Reduced integration issues
- ✅ Improved product quality

---

## 🔹 ✅ Key Takeaways
- Use **Waterfall as the backbone**
- Treat Agile efforts as **integrated components**
- Deliver Agile features **incrementally**
- Allocate time for:
  - Integration
  - Testing
- Continuously align:
  - Agile backlog with Waterfall schedule
- Clear roles and communication are critical

---
# 📘 Study Notes: Challenge – Applying Agile to a Project

## 🔹 1. Objective of the Challenge
- Identify **which parts of a project** are suitable for:
  - ✅ Agile approach  
  - ❌ Waterfall approach  

✅ Goal:
- Use Agile where it provides **maximum value and flexibility**

---

## 🔹 2. When Agile Works Best

Agile is most effective when:

- Requirements are **uncertain or changing**
- Work can be delivered in **small increments**
- Frequent **feedback is needed**
- Value can be delivered **early and continuously**

---

## 🔹 3. Criteria to Identify Agile Candidates

Use the following checklist:

### ✅ Suitable for Agile if:
- Work can be broken into **features**
- Deliverables can be released **incrementally**
- High level of **user interaction** required
- Frequent **changes are expected**
- Continuous **testing and feedback** is beneficial

---

### ❌ Not Suitable for Agile if:
- Work is:
  - Fixed and well-defined
  - Heavily regulated
  - Dependent on strict sequence (e.g., infrastructure setup)
- Requires:
  - Detailed upfront planning
  - Minimal change

---

## 🔹 4. Typical Agile Candidates (Hospital Scenario)

### ✅ Strong Agile Candidates:
- User interface (UI) design and improvements  
- Scheduling features (booking, updates, changes)  
- Reporting features  
- User documentation  
- Staff training modules  

👉 Why?
- Can be developed in **small, usable increments**
- Benefit from **frequent user feedback**

---

### ⚖️ Partial Agile Candidates:
- Integration with existing systems  
- Workflow improvements  

👉 Approach:
- Use **hybrid** (Agile for development + Waterfall for integration)

---

### ❌ Better for Waterfall:
- Hardware installation  
- Network setup  
- Infrastructure configuration  
- Regulatory compliance tasks  

👉 Why?
- Require **sequential execution**
- Less flexibility for iterative changes

---

## 🔹 5. Approach to Selecting Agile Areas

### Step 1: Analyze each project area
- Identify type of work

### Step 2: Evaluate against Agile criteria
- Can it be iterative?
- Does it require flexibility?

### Step 3: Categorize
- Agile
- Waterfall
- Hybrid

---

## 🔹 6. Benefits of Using Agile Where Appropriate
- ✅ Faster delivery of usable features
- ✅ Improved customer satisfaction
- ✅ Flexibility to adapt to changes
- ✅ Better risk management through iterations

---

## 🔹 7. Risks of Misapplying Agile
- Using Agile for:
  - Fixed-scope tasks → Causes confusion
- Lack of integration planning → Causes delays
- Poor coordination with Waterfall → Misalignment

---

## 🔹 ✅ Key Takeaways
- Not all project areas should use Agile
- Select Agile for:
  - Flexible, feature-based work
- Use Waterfall for:
  - Structured, sequential tasks
- Hybrid approach balances:
  - Flexibility + control
- Proper selection improves:
  - Efficiency
  - Project success

---
