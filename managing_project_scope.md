# 📘 Study Notes: Managing Project Scope

## 🔹 1. What Is Project Scope?
- **Project scope** defines:
  - What work is included
  - What deliverables will be produced

✅ A clear scope:
- Sets expectations
- Prevents confusion during execution

---

## 🔹 2. What Is Scope Creep?
- **Scope creep** = uncontrolled expansion of project scope

### Example:
- Stakeholder casually requests:
  - “Can you add this feature?”
- No adjustment to:
  - Budget ❌
  - Schedule ❌

👉 If accepted informally → scope creep occurs

---

## 🔹 3. Why Scope Creep Happens

### ✅ 3.1 Stakeholder Requests
- New ideas emerge during the project

---

### ✅ 3.2 Poor Scope Definition
- Requirements not clearly documented upfront

---

### ✅ 3.3 Assumptions
- Customer assumes:
  - Team already understands all needs

---

### ✅ 3.4 Unclear Requirements
- Customer is unsure about:
  - What they actually want

---

## 🔹 4. Preventing Scope Creep

### ✅ Best Approach:
- Clearly define scope during planning

### Include:
- Scope statement
- Detailed requirements
- Project boundaries

---

## 🔹 5. Managing Scope After Planning

If scope starts expanding, take action:

---

### ✅ 5.1 Reset Expectations
- Politely point out:
  - Requests are outside approved scope

Example:
    "This feature is not included in the current scope."

---

### ✅ 5.2 Explain Impact
- Link new requests to:
  - Schedule changes
  - Budget increases

✅ Helps stakeholders understand trade-offs

---

### ✅ 5.3 Enforce Change Management
- No informal changes allowed

### Rule:
- All changes must go through:
  - **Formal change request process**

✅ Keeps control over scope

---

### ✅ 5.4 Clarify or Renegotiate Scope
- If scope was unclear:
  - Work with customer to refine it

---

### ✅ 5.5 Use Agile When Needed
- If requirements are evolving:

👉 Switch to Agile approach:
- Iterative delivery
- Continuous refinement

---

## 🔹 6. Warning Signs of Scope Creep

- Frequent informal requests
- Increasing workload without timeline updates
- Confusion about deliverables
- Stakeholders bypassing processes

---

## 🔹 7. Impact of Poor Scope Management

- Delays in project completion
- Budget overruns
- Overloaded team
- Reduced quality

---

## 🔹 8. Best Practices

- Define scope clearly early
- Communicate scope to all stakeholders
- Use formal change control process
- Track and document all changes
- Educate stakeholders about impact

---

## 🔹 ✅ Key Takeaways
- Scope creep = uncontrolled addition of work
- Prevent it with **clear planning and communication**
- Enforce **change management process**
- Always link changes to:
  - Time
  - Cost
- Agile is helpful when scope is uncertain
- Strong scope control is essential for project success

---

# 📘 Study Notes: Managing and Controlling Project Risks

## 🔹 1. Purpose of Risk Management During Execution
- Risk management continues after planning
- Goal:
  - Monitor risks
  - Respond when risks occur

✅ Key Idea:
- Be **proactive before risks happen** and **responsive when they do**

---

## 🔹 2. Risk Ownership

### ✅ Risk Owner:
- Each risk is assigned to a responsible person

### Responsibilities:
- Monitor the risk
- Update status regularly
- Execute response plans when needed

---

## 🔹 3. Managing Risks Step-by-Step

## ✅ 3.1 Before the Risk Occurs (Proactive Phase)

### Actions:
- Implement planned responses:
  - **Avoid** → eliminate the risk
  - **Mitigate** → reduce likelihood or impact
  - **Contingency Plan** → prepare fallback actions

✅ Goal:
- Reduce or prevent risk impact

---

## ✅ 3.2 Monitor Risks

### Watch for:
- Early warning signs
- Trigger events (signals that a risk is about to occur)

✅ Focus on:
- High-priority risks first

---

## ✅ 3.3 When Risk Occurs (Reactive Phase)

### Actions:
- Execute the planned response
- Apply contingency plan if needed

✅ Goal:
- Minimize damage to project

---

## ✅ 3.4 Track Results
- Evaluate:
  - Effectiveness of response
- Adjust actions if necessary

---

## ✅ 3.5 Report and Update

### Update:
- Risk log
- Risk detail sheets

### Include:
- Current status
- Changes in:
  - Probability
  - Impact

---

## ✅ 3.6 Close Risks
- If risk:
  - No longer relevant
  - Has passed

✅ Action:
- Mark it **closed**

---

## 🔹 4. Managing Low-Priority Risks

- Do not ignore them

### ✅ Actions:
- Periodically review
- Reassess:
  - Probability
  - Impact

✅ Reason:
- Project changes can increase their importance

---

## 🔹 5. Identify New Risks

- As project evolves:
  - New risks may emerge

### ✅ Action:
- Continuously scan for:
  - New threats
- Add them to:
  - Risk log

---

## 🔹 6. Key Risk Management Cycle

    Identify → Assess → Monitor → Respond → Update → Close

---

## 🔹 7. Best Practices

- Assign clear ownership for each risk
- Focus on high-priority risks
- Act quickly when risks materialize
- Keep documentation updated
- Review risks regularly
- Stay proactive and vigilant

---

## 🔹 ✅ Key Takeaways
- Risk management is ongoing throughout the project
- Risk owners play a critical role
- Prepare responses **before risks occur**
- Act immediately when risks happen
- Continuously monitor, update, and close risks
- Stay alert for new and evolving risks

# 📘 Study Notes: Earned Value Analysis (EVA)

## 🔹 1. What Is Earned Value Analysis?
- **Earned Value Analysis (EVA)** measures:
  - ✅ Schedule performance  
  - ✅ Cost performance  

✅ Key Idea:
- A project “earns value” by **completing work**

---

## 🔹 2. Why Use Earned Value Analysis?

### ⚠️ Problem with Simple Metrics:
- Example:
  - 50% time used ✅
  - 50% budget spent ✅
  - Only 25% work complete ❌

👉 This looks OK but is actually:
- Behind schedule
- At risk of budget overrun

---

### ✅ EVA Solves This By:
- Comparing:
  - Work completed
  - Work planned
  - Money spent

---

## 🔹 3. Core EVA Measures

## ✅ 3.1 Planned Value (PV)
- Also called:
  - **Budgeted Cost of Work Scheduled (BCWS)**

### Definition:
- Amount of money **planned to be spent** by the status date

### Example:
- Planned spending by May 12:
  - $5,000 → PV = $5,000

---

## ✅ 3.2 Earned Value (EV)
- Also called:
  - **Budgeted Cost of Work Performed (BCWP)**

### Definition:
- Value of **work actually completed**, expressed in money

### Example:
- Work completed = 30 hours
- Planned cost of work = $3,000  
→ EV = $3,000

---

## ✅ 3.3 Actual Cost (AC)
- Also called:
  - **Actual Cost of Work Performed (ACWP)**

### Definition:
- Actual money spent to complete work

### Example:
- Actual labor cost = $3,500  
→ AC = $3,500

---

## 🔹 4. Interpreting EVA

## ✅ Schedule Performance

Compare:
    EV vs PV

- EV > PV → ✅ Ahead of schedule  
- EV < PV → ❌ Behind schedule  

---

## ✅ Cost Performance

Compare:
    EV vs AC

- EV > AC → ✅ Under budget  
- EV < AC → ❌ Over budget  

---

## 🔹 5. Reading an EVA Graph

### Axes:
- Horizontal → Time
- Vertical → Cost

---

### ✅ Ideal Situation:
- EV above PV → Ahead of schedule
- AC below EV → Under budget

---

### ✅ Example Scenario:

- EV < PV → ❌ Behind schedule  
- AC < EV → ✅ Under budget  

👉 Meaning:
- Work is slower than planned
- But spending is under control

---

## 🔹 6. Summary Relationships

    Schedule Status:
    EV - PV

    Cost Status:
    EV - AC

---

## 🔹 7. When to Use EVA

- During **project execution**
- When tracking:
  - Cost performance
  - Schedule performance

✅ Especially useful for:
- Large or complex projects
- Government or regulated projects

---

## 🔹 8. Benefits of EVA

- ✅ Provides objective performance measurement  
- ✅ Detects issues early  
- ✅ Combines cost + schedule insights  
- ✅ Supports better decision-making  

---

## 🔹 ✅ Key Takeaways
- EVA measures project performance using:
  - Planned Value (PV)
  - Earned Value (EV)
  - Actual Cost (AC)
- EV vs PV → tells schedule status  
- EV vs AC → tells cost status  
- Helps uncover hidden problems early  
- Essential for accurate project monitoring and control

# 📘 Study Notes: Evaluating Project Progress

## 🔹 1. Purpose of Progress Evaluation
- During execution, you must continuously evaluate:
  - ✅ Schedule performance  
  - ✅ Cost performance  

✅ Goal:
- Determine:
  - Where the project stands
  - What issues need attention

---

## 🔹 2. Evaluating Schedule Performance

### ✅ Use a Gantt Chart
- Compare:
  - **Current schedule** vs **Baseline schedule**

---

### ✅ How to Interpret:

- Task bars **ahead of baseline** → ✅ Ahead of schedule  
- Task bars **aligned with baseline** → ✅ On schedule  
- Task bars **behind baseline** → ❌ Behind schedule  

---

### ✅ Example:
- Implementation tasks occur **later than baseline**
→ Project is **behind schedule**

---

## 🔹 3. Schedule Variance

- Shows difference between:
  - Planned vs actual dates

### Example:
    Finish Variance = -25 days

👉 Meaning:
- Project is 25 days behind  
- Need to recover 25 days to stay on track

---

## 🔹 4. Identifying Schedule Issues

### ✅ Look for:
- Tasks that:
  - Haven’t started but should have
  - Are incomplete and behind
  - Have fewer work hours completed than planned

✅ Benefit:
- Early warning of delays

---

## 🔹 5. Evaluating Cost Performance

- Compare:
  - **Actual costs** vs **Budgeted costs**

---

## 🔹 6. Cost Variance Analysis

### ✅ If Over Budget:
Possible causes:
- Scope creep (extra work added)
- Tasks taking longer than expected
- Higher resource costs

---

### ✅ If Appears Over Budget Temporarily:
- Work progressing faster than planned

👉 Note:
- Costs may stabilize later if:
  - Work stays within total estimate

---

## 🔹 7. Key Questions to Ask

### Schedule:
- Are tasks on time?
- Which tasks are causing delays?

### Cost:
- Are we within budget?
- Why are costs higher or lower?

---

## 🔹 8. Identifying Problem Areas

### Focus on:
- Tasks behind schedule
- Tasks with high cost overruns
- Critical path activities

✅ Why?
- These have the greatest impact on project success

---

## 🔹 9. Next Steps After Evaluation

- Identify root causes of issues
- Develop corrective actions:
  - Adjust schedule
  - Reallocate resources
  - Control scope

---

## 🔹 ✅ Key Takeaways
- Compare **current vs baseline** to track progress
- Gantt charts show schedule performance visually
- Variance values quantify delays
- Monitor both:
  - Schedule
  - Cost
- Identify issues early to take corrective action
- Data-driven evaluation keeps projects on track

---
# 📘 Study Notes: Choosing an Approach for Evaluating Project Progress

## 🔹 1. Overview
- There are multiple ways to evaluate project progress
- Selection depends on:
  - ✅ Available data
  - ✅ Level of accuracy required
  - ✅ Tools/software available

✅ Key Trade-off:
- Simplicity vs Accuracy

---

## 🔹 2. Progress Evaluation Methods

## ✅ 2.1 Tracking Completed Milestones

### 📌 Requirements:
- Task plan with clear milestones

### 📊 Data Needed:
- Planned vs actual milestone completion dates

### ⚙️ Approach:
- Compare planned vs actual dates

---

### ✅ Pros:
- Simple and meaningful progress indicator  
- Easy to communicate  

### ❌ Cons:
- Does NOT show future performance  
- Ignores effort and cost  

---

## ✅ 2.2 Tracking Number of Completed Tasks

### 📌 Requirements:
- All tasks defined upfront

### 📊 Data Needed:
- Completion status of tasks

### ⚙️ Approach:
    % Complete = Completed Tasks / Total Tasks

---

### ✅ Pros:
- Very simple and quick  
- No special tools required  

### ❌ Cons:
- Tasks vary in size → misleading progress  
- No view of future schedule or cost  

---

## ✅ 2.3 Monitoring with Gantt Chart

### 📌 Requirements:
- Defined tasks with durations and dependencies
- Project management software

### 📊 Data Needed:
- Updated start/end dates
- Progress (duration/work completed)

### ⚙️ Approach:
- Compare actual vs planned schedule visually

---

### ✅ Pros:
- Visual and easy to understand  
- Shows schedule delays and dependencies  

### ❌ Cons:
- Requires understanding of Gantt charts  
- Does NOT track cost  

---

## ✅ 2.4 Tracking Actual Cost

### 📌 Requirements:
- Cost plan
- Labor and expense tracking

### 📊 Data Needed:
- Staff hours
- Cost of labor and materials

### ⚙️ Approach:
- Compare actual spending vs planned budget

---

### ✅ Pros:
- Strong insight into **financial performance**  
- Important for cost-focused projects  

### ❌ Cons:
- Does NOT show schedule performance  
- Delays may increase future costs  

---

## ✅ 2.5 Tracking the Critical Path

### 📌 Requirements:
- Full task schedule with dependencies
- Software to calculate critical path

### 📊 Data Needed:
- Task progress and actual dates

### ⚙️ Approach:
- Compare actual vs planned progress on critical tasks

---

### ✅ Pros:
- Shows:
  - Project completion date forecast  
  - Key delays affecting schedule  

### ❌ Cons:
- Requires tools and expertise  
- Does NOT include cost tracking  

---

## ✅ 2.6 Earned Value Management (EVM)

### 📌 Requirements:
- Detailed schedule and cost plan
- Software support
- Accurate tracking system

### 📊 Data Needed:
- Task progress
- Actual costs
- Planned costs
- Labor hours

### ⚙️ Approach:
- Compare:
  - Work completed vs planned
  - Cost spent vs planned

---

### ✅ Pros:
- Most comprehensive method  
- Tracks:
  - Schedule + Cost performance  
- Provides:
  - Forecasts for completion time and cost  

---

### ❌ Cons:
- Requires large amount of data  
- Needs specialized tools  
- Dependent on timely financial reporting  

---

## 🔹 3. Comparison Summary

| Method                | Complexity | Tracks Schedule | Tracks Cost | Forecast Capability |
|----------------------|-----------|----------------|------------|---------------------|
| Milestones           | Low       | ✅              | ❌          | ❌                   |
| Task Completion      | Low       | ✅              | ❌          | ❌                   |
| Gantt Chart          | Medium    | ✅              | ❌          | Limited             |
| Actual Cost          | Medium    | ❌              | ✅          | Limited             |
| Critical Path        | High      | ✅              | ❌          | ✅                   |
| Earned Value (EVM)   | High      | ✅              | ✅          | ✅✅                  |

---

## 🔹 4. How to Choose the Right Approach

### ✅ Use Simple Methods When:
- Project is small
- Limited tools or data available

---

### ✅ Use Intermediate Methods When:
- Need better schedule visibility
- Tools like Gantt charts are available

---

### ✅ Use Advanced Methods When:
- Project is:
  - Large
  - Complex
  - High-risk
- Accurate forecasting is required

---

## 🔹 5. Key Selection Factors

- Availability of:
  - Data
  - Tools
- Project size and complexity
- Stakeholder requirements
- Need for:
  - Forecasting accuracy

---

## 🔹 ✅ Key Takeaways
- No single method fits all projects
- Simpler methods are easier but less accurate
- Advanced methods provide:
  - Deeper insights
  - Better forecasting
- Choose based on:
  - Project needs
  - Data availability
  - Organizational capability

# 📘 Study Notes: How to Get a Project Back on Track

## 🔹 1. When a Project Goes Off Track
- Projects may fall behind due to:
  - Schedule delays
  - Cost overruns
  - Scope issues

✅ Goal:
- Identify corrective actions and **restore performance**

---

## 🔹 2. Step 1: Identify the Root Problem
- Determine:
  - Is the issue:
    - ⏱️ Schedule-related?
    - 💰 Cost-related?
    - 📦 Scope-related?

✅ Focus efforts on the **primary constraint**

---

## 🔹 3. Step 2: Select Recovery Techniques

## ✅ 3.1 If Behind Schedule

### Options:
- **Fast Tracking**
  - Overlap tasks
- **Crashing**
  - Add resources (increase cost)
- **Overtime**
  - Extend working hours

✅ Focus:
- Tasks on the **critical path**

---

## ✅ 3.2 If Over Budget

### Options:
- Use:
  - Less expensive resources
- Reduce:
  - Non-essential costs

---

## ✅ 3.3 Reduce Scope (Last Resort)
- Remove or defer deliverables

✅ Impact:
- Shortens:
  - Schedule
  - Cost

⚠️ Trade-off:
- Reduced project value

---

## 🔹 4. Step 3: Use Authority Wisely

### ✅ Implement Changes Without Approval (if allowed):
- Reassign resources
- Adjust task sequencing

✅ Benefit:
- Faster action

---

### ✅ Seek Approval When Needed

Approval required for:
- Extending schedule
- Increasing budget
- Reducing scope

---

## 🔹 5. Step 4: Present Recommendations

### Include:
- Proposed solutions
- Pros and cons of each option

### Be prepared to:
- Answer questions
- Justify your recommendation
- Explain rejected alternatives

---

## 🔹 6. Step 5: Escalate if Necessary

### When to involve higher management:
- Need additional:
  - Budget (contingency funds)
  - Resources from other projects

✅ May require approval from:
- Senior management or executive board

---

## 🔹 7. Example Recovery Strategy

### Situation:
- Project is behind schedule

### Possible approach:
- Fast track selected critical tasks  
- Add limited overtime  
- Reassign internal resources  

### Approval:
- No approval needed for reassignment  
- Approval needed for:
  - Overtime budget increase  

---

## 🔹 8. Key Principles

- Act quickly but thoughtfully  
- Focus on **critical path**  
- Choose **least disruptive solution first**  
- Balance:
  - Time
  - Cost
  - Scope  

---

## 🔹 ✅ Key Takeaways
- Projects going off track is normal  
- Use techniques like:
  - Fast tracking
  - Crashing
  - Cost reduction
  - Scope adjustment  
- Start with actions within your authority  
- Seek approval for major changes  
- Present clear recommendations with trade-offs  
- Effective recovery keeps the project aligned with goals

# 📘 Study Notes: Challenge – Managing a Change Request (Plan Updates)

## 🔹 1. Scenario Summary
- Vendor procurement delays → Project finish date exceeds **grant expiration**
- High priority issue (financial risk)
- ✅ Change request approved:
  - Vendor will perform additional work to resolve delay

---

## 🔹 2. Objective
- Identify which project plans/tools must be updated
- Define **what changes are required**

---

## 🔹 3. Plans and Tools to Update

## ✅ 3.1 Project Schedule

### Required Updates:
- Add new tasks for:
  - Vendor work
  - Integration activities
- Update:
  - Task dependencies
  - Task durations
  - Start and finish dates

### Key Goal:
- Bring **project completion before grant expiration**

---

## ✅ 3.2 Budget (Cost Plan)

### Required Updates:
- Include:
  - Vendor costs (contract, fees)
- Adjust:
  - Total project budget
- Recalculate:
  - Cost estimates for affected tasks

### Key Consideration:
- Ensure budget still aligns with **funding constraints**

---

## ✅ 3.3 Resource Plan

### Required Updates:
- Add:
  - Vendor resources
- Adjust:
  - Internal resource assignments
  - Workload distribution

### Key Goal:
- Ensure proper coordination between:
  - Internal team
  - Vendor team

---

## ✅ 3.4 Risk Management Plan

### Required Updates:
- Add new risks:
  - Vendor performance risk
  - Integration risk
  - Dependency risks
- Update:
  - Risk probability and impact
- Define:
  - Mitigation and contingency plans

---

## ✅ 3.5 Communication Plan

### Required Updates:
- Include vendor in:
  - Communication structure
  - Reporting processes
- Define:
  - Status reporting frequency
  - Escalation paths

### Key Goal:
- Improve coordination and prevent miscommunication

---

## ✅ 3.6 Quality Plan

### Required Updates:
- Define:
  - Quality standards for vendor deliverables
- Add:
  - Acceptance criteria
  - Review and testing processes

### Key Goal:
- Ensure vendor work meets project quality requirements

---

## 🔹 4. Additional Updates (Best Practice)

### ✅ Baseline Documents
- Update:
  - Schedule baseline
  - Cost baseline
- Tag all changes with:
  - Change Request ID

---

### ✅ Change Request Log
- Update:
  - Status
  - Impact details
  - Final outcomes

---

## 🔹 5. Key Considerations

- Ensure:
  - Alignment across all plans
- Monitor:
  - Vendor performance closely
- Protect:
  - Critical path tasks
- Validate:
  - Project meets grant deadline

---

## 🔹 6. Summary of Impact

| Plan/Tool            | Key Changes                                   |
|---------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Schedule            | Add vendor tasks, update timeline            |
| Budget              | Include vendor costs, adjust total budget    |
| Resource Plan       | Add vendor resources, rebalance workload     |
| Risk Plan           | Add vendor-related risks                     |
| Communication Plan  | Include vendor communication processes       |
| Quality Plan        | Define vendor quality standards              |

---

## 🔹 ✅ Key Takeaways
- Approved change requests impact **multiple project plans**
- Always update:
  - Schedule, cost, resources, and risks
- Include vendors in:
  - Communication and quality processes
- Maintain:
  - Baseline integrity and traceability
- Holistic updates ensure:
  - Smooth implementation
  - Project success despite changes
