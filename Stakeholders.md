## 2. Tools to Identify Stakeholder Needs & Expectations

### üõ† Tool 1: Stakeholder Analysis (Power‚ÄìInterest Grid)

A visualization with **four quadrants**:

| Power | Interest | Approach |
|-------|----------|----------|
| High | High | **Manage Closely** ‚Äî frequent engagement and communication |
| High | Low | **Keep Satisfied** ‚Äî inform without overwhelming |
| Low | High | **Keep Informed** ‚Äî update regularly, address concerns |
| Low | Low | **Monitor** ‚Äî respond to issues as needed |

**Steps:**
1. Assess each stakeholder‚Äôs *power* and *interest*.
2. Place them into the correct quadrant.
3. Tailor communication accordingly.

---

### üõ† Tool 2: Stakeholder Engagement Assessment Matrix

Helps determine:
- **Current engagement level**
- **Desired engagement level**

Common engagement categories:
- Unaware  
- Resistant  
- Neutral  
- Supportive  
- Leading  

**Example:**  
- Stakeholder 3:  
  - *Current:* Unaware  
  - *Desired:* Supportive  

Use this gap to plan communication and engagement strategies.

## 3. Using Analysis to Manage Stakeholders
Once stakeholder information is collected:
- Develop a **communication plan**.
- Maintain engagement throughout the project.
- Continuously ensure expectations are addressed.

**Note:** Stakeholder management requires:
- Patience  
- Time  
- Active relationship-building  

But the payoff is a more successful project.

## 4. Identifying the Right Resources

### üß∞ What Are Project Resources?
Resources include:

- Equipment  
- Supplies  
- Materials  
- Capital  

Effective resource planning prevents project delays and bottlenecks.

---

## 5. Steps for Effective Resource Planning

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ Understand Project Scope
- Define and agree on scope.
- Break down the work using a **Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)**.

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ Identify Needed Resources
Use tools like a **Resource Breakdown Structure (RBS)**:
- Internal resources  
- External resources  
- Categorization prompts (e.g., human, material, equipment)

Ask:
- What skills are needed?
- How many resources?
- What specs (size, power)?
- When are they needed?
- Are they available?
- What % of their time can be committed?

### 3Ô∏è‚É£ Estimate Resource Time
Common estimating techniques:

| Technique | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Bottom-Up** | Estimate at lowest level, then aggregate upward |
| **Analogous** | Estimate using similar historical projects |
| **Parametric** | Use formulas based on historical data (e.g., cost per mile) |

### 4Ô∏è‚É£ Assign Resources to Tasks
- Coordinate with HR or functional managers.
- Work with procurement for physical resources.
- Ensure proper timing, quantity, and skill match.

### 5Ô∏è‚É£ Monitor Resources During Execution
- Regularly look ahead several weeks.
- Identify:
  - Who begins work soon
  - Required materials/equipment
  - Potential bottlenecks
- Adjust proactively to avoid delays.
