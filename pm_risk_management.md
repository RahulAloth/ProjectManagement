# 📘 Identifying Risks – Study Notes

## ⚠️ What is Risk in a Project?

A **risk** is any uncertain event that could negatively impact:
- Project timeline  
- Cost  
- Scope  
- Quality  

📌 Key principle:
> Things *will* go wrong — planning ahead reduces impact.

---

## 🔍 Types of Risks

### 📌 1. Known Unknowns
- Risks you can **anticipate**
- Examples:
  - Weather delays  
  - Resource unavailability  
  - Technology issues  

✅ These can be planned for in advance.

---

### 📌 2. Unknown Unknowns
- Risks you **cannot predict**
- Rare or unexpected events

📌 Example:
- Highly unlikely or unprecedented events

✅ Managed by:
- Adding **contingency time and budget**

---

## ⚡ Common Sources of Project Risk

### 🖥️ Technology Risks
- Systems may:
  - Fail  
  - Be delayed  
  - Cost more than expected  

---

### 🌍 Distributed Teams
- Time zone differences  
- Cultural/language barriers  
- Weak team cohesion  

---

### 📄 Lack of Detail
- Unclear deliverables  
- Undefined deadlines  
- Missing task ownership  

---

### 👨‍🔧 Limited Resources
- Specialized skills are hard to replace  
- Few alternatives if issues arise  

---

## 👥 How to Identify Risks

Work collaboratively with:

- ✅ Project team members  
- ✅ Subject matter experts  
- ✅ Stakeholders  
- ✅ Other project managers (based on similar projects)  

📌 Brainstorm and gather input from multiple perspectives.

---

## 📝 Risk Information Form

For each identified risk, document:

- 🎯 **Objectives affected**  
- ⚡ **Risk event** (what could go wrong)  
- 📉 **Consequences** (impact)  
- 👤 **Risk owner** (who is responsible)  
- 📌 Additional details as needed  

---

## 💰 Contingency Planning

To handle unknown risks:
- Set aside:
  - **Extra time**
  - **Extra budget (contingency funds)**  

📊 Common practice:
- Allocate around **15% of project budget**  
- Adjust based on past experience  

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> You can’t prevent all risks, but identifying them early allows you to reduce their impact significantly.

---

## 🚀 Key Takeaways

- Identify both **known and unknown risks**
- Analyze risks from:
  - Technology  
  - Team structure  
  - Resources  
- Use **team collaboration** to uncover risks  
- Document risks clearly using a structured format  
- Always include **contingency reserves**

---
# 📘 Risk Management Plan – Study Notes

## 🧩 What is a Risk Management Plan?

A **Risk Management Plan** documents:
- Which risks to focus on  
- How those risks will be handled  
- How risks will be monitored and controlled  

📌 Goal:
> Minimize the negative impact of risks on the project.

---

## 🔍 Step 1: Evaluate & Prioritize Risks

### 📊 Risk Scoring Method

1. Rate each risk on a scale of **1–5**:
   - **Probability** (likelihood of occurring)
   - **Impact** (severity of consequences)

2. Calculate:
``` Text
Risk Score = Probability × Impact

```

---

### ✅ Which Risks to Manage?

- Focus on risks with a score of **9 or higher**
- These are:
  - Likely to happen  
  - Have serious impact  

📌 Example:
- Probability = 5 (very likely)  
- Impact = 5 (very serious)  
- Risk Score = 25 → MUST manage  

---

## 🛠️ Step 2: Plan Risk Responses

A **risk response** defines how to handle each risk.

### 🔹 1. Accept
- Do nothing  
- Used for low-impact, low-probability risks  

---

### 🔹 2. Avoid
- Eliminate the risk entirely  
- Example: Change project scope  

---

### 🔹 3. Mitigate
- Reduce likelihood or impact  
- Example: Conduct feasibility studies  

---

### 🔹 4. Transfer
- Shift risk to another party  
- Example: Insurance or outsourcing  

📌 Note:
- Reduces responsibility, but does NOT eliminate the risk  

---

## ⚖️ Cost vs Benefit Principle

- Avoid overreacting to risks

📌 Example:
- Do NOT spend €5000 to avoid a €500 penalty  

---

## 💰 Contingency Planning

- Reserve:
  - Extra **budget**
  - Extra **time**

✅ Used for:
- Risks without a clear response  
- Unexpected (unknown) risks  

---

## 📋 Step 3: Monitor & Control Risks

### 📌 Use a Risk Log

A **Risk Log** includes:

- 📝 Risk description  
- ⚡ Trigger events (what causes the risk)  
- 📊 Probability & impact  
- 🛠️ Selected response  
- 👤 Risk owner (monitoring responsibility)  
- 🎯 Expected outcome of response  
- 📌 Risk status (active, resolved, etc.)  

---

## 🔄 Ongoing Risk Management

- Continuously update the plan:
  - Some risks decrease over time  
  - New risks may emerge  

- Track:
  - What caused risks  
  - Their impact  
  - Effectiveness of responses  

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> Risk management is a continuous process—not a one-time activity.

---

## 🚀 Key Takeaways

- Evaluate risks using **probability × impact**
- Focus on **high-priority risks (≥9 score)**
- Choose appropriate responses:
  - Accept, Avoid, Mitigate, Transfer  
- Use **contingency reserves** for uncertainty  
- Track risks using a **risk log**
- Continuously monitor and adapt your plan

# 📘 Tips for Documenting Risks & Mitigations – Study Notes

## ⚠️ Why Risk Documentation Matters

Proper risk documentation helps:
- ✅ Anticipate potential problems  
- ✅ Prepare effective responses  
- ✅ Track risks throughout the project  
- ✅ Minimize overall project impact  

---

## 📝 Tips for Documenting Risks

---

### 🔹 1. Use the PIE Model

Each risk should include:

- **P (Probability)** → Likelihood of occurrence  
- **I (Impact)** → Consequences if it happens  
- **E (Event)** → What triggers the risk  

📌 Example:
Instead of one generic risk like *“late delivery”*, define multiple risks:

- Driver illness  
- Truck breakdown  
- Company failure  

✅ Why?
- Each event may require a **different response**

---

### 🔹 2. Identify Trigger Events

- Define early warning signs that a risk may occur

📌 Examples:
- Missed intermediate deadlines  
- Delayed supplier updates  
- Resource unavailability signals  

✅ Helps you act **before the risk fully materializes**

---

### 🔹 3. Integrate Risk Checks into the WBS

- Add tasks in the **Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)** to track risks

📌 Examples:
- Verify delivery status before deadline  
- Check readiness of systems before integration  
- Confirm availability of critical resources  

✅ Ensures risks are **actively monitored**, not forgotten

---

## 🛠️ Tips for Documenting Risk Responses

---

### 🔹 1. Assign Ownership

- Assign a **clear owner** for each risk response  

📌 Define:
- Who executes the response  
- When it should be triggered  

---

### 🔹 2. Define Success Criteria

- Specify how you’ll know if the response is working  

📌 Example:
- Reduced delay  
- Lower cost impact  
- Improved system performance  

---

### 🔹 3. Avoid Generic Responses

- Do NOT use standard project practices as risk responses  

🚫 Example:
- “Improve communication” → Not a valid risk response  

✅ Risk responses must be:
- Specific  
- Actionable  
- Directly tied to the risk  

---

### 🔹 4. Evaluate Secondary Risks

- Risk responses may create **new risks**

📌 Example:
- Hiring a contractor → risk of data confidentiality issues  

✅ Always:
- Identify and manage **new risks created by responses**

---

## 🔄 Continuous Risk Tracking

- Monitor risks throughout the project lifecycle  
- Update documentation regularly  
- Track:
  - Risk status  
  - Trigger events  
  - Response effectiveness  

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> Good risk documentation doesn’t just list risks — it connects events, impacts, triggers, and actionable responses.

---

## 🚀 Key Takeaways

- Use the **PIE model (Probability, Impact, Event)**  
- Break down risks into **specific, actionable events**  
- Identify **early warning triggers**  
- Include **risk tracking tasks in the WBS**  
- Assign **clear ownership for responses**  
- Define **measurable success criteria**  
- Watch for **new risks caused by responses**  
- Continuously **monitor and update risks**
